JAPAN

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove & Monkey Park - Kyoto, Japan

24/7 entrance and free admission! Say no more. The Arashiyama Bamboo Grove is a beautiful place to spend some time. My family and I went around 12-1pm and there were several others there at the same time. There are endless rows of towering natural bamboo. Many say dawn or dusk is the best time for viewing the bamboo forest. The bamboo forest is a major tourist attraction, so prepare for there to be a lot of people from near and far there. The walkthrough the entire bamboo forest took about 30-45 minutes, including restroom breaks. The restrooms are located at the end of the path, before turning around. The bamboo forest was a walk to remember. I recommend attending the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove if you are planning on traveling to this part of Japan. The Tenryū-ji temple and the famous Nonomiya Shrine are not far from the forest.

Hours: 9-4 Daily. Admission fee is 600 yen (aprox. $4). This park is an amazing experience!! It is a 15-20 minute walk up hill over 100 steps to make it to the top of the monkey park. During the walk up the mountain (about 160m high) you might encounter a few monkeys wandering around the path. The majority of the monkeys will be at the top of the mountain. There were around 120 wild monkeys when I went in March of 2024. They were playing with each other, swinging from trees, drinking from the koi pond, and sun bathing. Many of the monkeys were cleaning each other, swinging from trees, and some were casually sitting and watching us humans being in awe of them. There are important rules to follow, the most important being do not stare into the monkeys eyes. They may get angry and think that you are looking to challenge them to a duel. Do not pet the monkeys, and do not feed them outside food. However, there is an option to buy food for them at the enterance for 100 yen.

Garmisch-Partenkirchen

My view from Edelweiss Lodge and Resort in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany. In Ettal, Germany I came across this beautiful church called Ettal Abbey. It is a Benediction monastery known for its stunning Baroque art, intricate frescoes, and stuccoes created by Johann Baptist Zimmerman and Johann Georg Üblhör. Garmisch-Partenkirchen is a magical Bavarian town featuring a small, cozy Christmas market with a large Christmas tree, holiday music, and stalls selling traditional crafts and food, alongside an outdoor ice-skating rink. Visitors can also enjoy holiday food and drinks, including glühwein (mulled wine).
